Nothing tastes better than a healthy, homemade meal, and that goes for baby purees as well.

Here are some of the homemade recipe for your little one.(6months+)



Carrot puree

Sterilize all the utensils needed for making the Puree either using a Sterilizer or boiling them in hot water. Clean the carrot with water and peel the skin. Chop the carrot into small cubes. Place the carrot cubes in a small bowl in cooker filled partially with water. Allow one to 2 Whistles. Switch off the flame. The carrots will be very soft. The steamed carrots can either be mashed or blended in a mixie Your Carrot Puree is ready. Breast milk or formula can be added to make it runny. Start with thin liquid like consistency and slowly make the puree thicker.



Pear puree

Peel and cut a pear into chunks so as to avoid the little seed portion. Steam gently until tender. Place in a blender/food processor and puree until smooth. You may be able to just use a fork instead. Add extra hot water if needed but pears tend to be very runny and watery without adding liquid - add some baby cereal to thicken if needed.



Ragi Porridge

Boil the milk (* boil only water for babies less than 12 months).Add 1 tbsp jaggery to boiling milk and stir well. Let it cook on low flame for 2 minutes.Add 50 ml of milk(for 12+ month baby) or water(for 6 to 12 month baby) to ragi powder, mix thoroughly until no lumps are formed.Keep on stirring and add ragi mix to it and cook for 2 minutes in low flame.Turn off the flame once bubbles are formed.Let it come down to room temperature and take it into feeding bowl and feed tasty jaggery ragi porridge



Moong daal Khichidi

Wash and soak the green moong dal and rice in enough water for 15 minutes. Drain and keep aside. Combine the green moong dal, rice, turmeric powder and 1 cup of water in a pressure cooker, mix well and pressure cook for 3 whistles. Allow the steam to escape before opening the lid. Mash the khichdi very well using a potato masher. Add the ghee and mix well. Serve the moong dal khichdi for babies with the lukewarm.



Pumpkin puree

Take a piece of pumpkin, deseed and remove the skin neatly. Wash it well and chop it. Add it to a pressure cooker or a pan and add water. Pressure cook it for 2 whistles or pan cook by closing a lid for about 10 - 15 minutes. Once cooked transfer the cooked pumpkin pieces to a blender jar and add a pinch of cinnamon powder. You can even use cumin or pepper powder to flavor the puree. Blend it to a fine puree and the Pumpkin puree is ready to be served.



Poha porridge

Heat jaggery in a pan with water till it immersing level. Switch off the flame once slight honey like consistency is reached. Strain the jaggery water using a filter to remove impurities. This step is completely optional. Take 2 tablespoon of poha cereal mix in a saucepan. Add ¾ cup of water and mix well with a whisk. Make sure there are no lumps remained. Cook the porridge for 5 minutes until it becomes slightly thick. Now Add ghee and jaggery syrup/salt at this stage. Give a quick stir. Cook it further for 2 minutes and switch off the flame. Cool it down and serve warm.



Sooji porridge

hod Take 2 tablespoon of roasted sooji in a saucepan to add the required amount of hot water and ghee to the sooji and mix everything slowly with a whisk. Make sure that there are no lumps formed. Cook the sooji in medium flame until the rava cooked completely. Stir it continuously while cooking otherwise porridge may stick to the vessel. Then add powdered organic jaggery/filtered jaggery water to the cooked sooji. Cook it further for 3 minutes or until it is thick and switch off the flame once done. The porridge becomes thick after cooling down. So switch off the flame accordingly. Once the porridge/kheer is warm, Add a cup of Warm boiled cow's milk/formula milk/breast milk to the cooked sooji. (Skip adding milk if you are preparing this porridge for a baby under one year ).
