Below are some of the home remedies for the little ones.

Breast Milk: If your baby is still being breastfed, there is really no better remedy to help treat your baby’s cold. Breast milk has the best balance of nutrients for immunity boosting and fighting infection over any other home remedy.
Cold compress: Placing a cold compress on the forehead and nape can efficiently reduce fever in toddlers. Add 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar to 4 tablespoons of cold water
Hydration: Encourage your baby to drink plenty of fluids to prevent dehydration.
Steam: Steam can help relieve congestion in babies. Run a hot shower and sit with your baby in the bathroom for 10-15 minutes
Chicken soup: Chicken soup can help alleviate cold symptoms in babies. It is rich in nutrients and helps boost the immune system
Honey: Honey can help soothe a sore throat in babies. Mix 1 teaspoon of honey with warm water and give it to your baby.
Elevate the head: Elevating your baby’s head can help relieve nasal congestion. Place a rolled-up towel under the head of the mattress to elevate it slightly
Lemons and Limes: Another good source of vitamin C, lemons and limes also help to cut through your baby’s cold symptoms as well. Like oranges lemons and limes have immunity boosters as well as antibiotic and anti-inflammatory properties to help relieve symptoms as well.
Onion: Raw, sliced onion can help break up congestion when your baby has a cold. Simply slice up an onion and place it on a plate next to the bed or crib. The sulfur content in the onion draws out mucus and fluids in the body .
Coconut oil: Coconut oil can be used to treat diaper rash. It has antibacterial and antifungal properties that can help prevent and treat infections
Chamomile tea: Chamomile tea can help soothe a colicky baby. It has a calming effect on the digestive system and can help reduce gas and bloating
Cinnamon: Cinnamon can help relieve diarrhea in babies. You can mix cinnamon powder with water and give it to your baby
Fennel: Fennel can help relieve colic in babies. You can give your baby fennel tea or mix fennel powder with water and give it to your baby
Ginger: Ginger can help relieve nausea and vomiting in babies. You can give your baby ginger tea or ginger ale, or mix ginger powder with honey and give it to your baby.
